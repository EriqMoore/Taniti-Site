About Taniti

Taniti is a small island located in the Pacific Ocean. It is known for its beautiful beaches, crystal clear waters, and lush tropical forests.

The island is home to a diverse range of plant and animal species, many of which are found nowhere else on Earth.

Taniti is also home to a rich cultural heritage, with a long history of traditional music, dance, and art. The island is a popular destination for tourists looking to relax and unwind in a peaceful and natural setting.

Travel Information

Getting Around

  • Tahiti has a bus system known as "Le Truck," along with standard public buses that operate on major routes around the island.
  • Taxis are available but can be expensive, especially at night. It's recommended to confirm fares in advance as they are not metered.
  • Visitors can rent cars from agencies near the airport and in Papeete, offering flexibility for exploring the island at their own pace.
  • Many areas, especially along the coast and in resort zones, are pedestrian-friendly, and bicycles can be rented for a scenic ride.

Visitor Essentials

  • U.S. citizens do not need a visa for stays under 90 days.
  • The local currency is the CFP franc (XPF), but major hotels and businesses accept credit cards.
  • French and Tahitian are the official languages, but English is spoken in most tourist areas.
  • Mosquito repellent is recommended due to the presence of mosquitoes, particularly in tropical areas.
